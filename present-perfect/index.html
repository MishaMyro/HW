Present Perfect

Use this homework to practise how we form and use the Present Perfect tense in everyday English.

Rule

We use the Present Perfect to connect the past with the present. The exact time is not important or is not mentioned.

subject + have / has + past participle

Positive

  • I have finished my homework.
  • She has visited London.
  • They have watched this film.

Negative

  • I have not finished my homework.
  • He has not cleaned his room.
  • We have not seen that teacher today.

Questions

  • Have you ever eaten sushi?
  • Has she done the task?
  • Have they arrived yet?

Useful words

ever never already yet just since for

Exercise 1: Complete the sentences

  5. They ________ (not / see) this film yet.
